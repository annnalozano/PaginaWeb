What is Soft Robotics?

Soft Robotics is a subfield of robotics that integrates compliant materials, soft and flexible, instead of rigid and stiff ones, into its fabrication. This shift in materiality allows the parts and links of robots to act and perform in various environments. It enables them to handle objects with great care, bringing them closer to human interaction.

Vinyl Method:

  1. Design the channel you want the air to go through in the balloon and cut this out of baking paper. Ensure there is an extended channel on one edge of the pattern to keep an opening for the air pump later.
  2. Cut out 2 pieces of vinyl bigger than the piece of baking paper in the overall shape you want for the balloon.
  3. Sandwich the baking paper between the vinyl with the film side of the vinyl on the outside. Ensure that the baking paper is in the middle of the 2 pieces, with the extended channel sticking out slightly.
  4. Heat press or iron the sandwich until the vinyl has completely fused, apart from the pattern of the baking paper.
  5. Add a straw in the extended channel of the baking paper and blow air in to inflate.

Silicone Method:

  1. Design the mold for the silicone in 2 parts. One part is a flat version of the overall shape of the inflatable, and the other is the same but with a raised path for the channel. Extend the channel to the edge of the shape to keep a channel for the air pump later.
  2. Laser cut in layers and assemble or 3D print the molds.
  3. Mix the silicone in a 1:1 ratio of parts A and B and pour it into the molds. Pour it slowly onto itself to minimize air bubbles.
  4. Leave to dry for 5 hours.
  5. Glue the 2 parts of the silicone together, being careful not to close off the air channels.
  6. Add an air pump in the channel left open and blow air in to inflate.

Stamped Methods

Glycerine:

Steps:

  1. Glue both parts with glycerin.
  2. Clamp it.
  3. Leave it for a few hours to ensure a good bond.

Comments: It works, but it's not very accurate; in some parts, the air escapes.

Heat Press:

Steps:

  1. Cut the desired shape with the laser cutter.
  2. In the heat press, adhere the following layers in this order: a piece of biomaterial, in the middle, parchment paper with the shape, and another piece of biomaterial. At a temperature not exceeding 35 degrees, place it in the heat press to fuse and vacuum-seal the shape along with the parchment paper.

Comments: It works better, also there are some parts where the air escapes but not as much.

Welding:
